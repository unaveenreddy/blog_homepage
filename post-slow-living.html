Slow Living in a Fast World

May 12, 2026 · 5 min read

The world runs on speed. Same-day delivery, instant messages, binge-watching, endless scrolling. We've optimised every corner of life for efficiency — and in the process, we've forgotten how to simply be.

What Slow Living Is Not

It's not about quitting your job, moving to a cabin, and growing your own vegetables (unless that's genuinely your thing). Slow living is a mindset: doing things at the right pace, with intention, rather than on autopilot.

The Attention Deficit

Our attention is the most valuable resource we have, and it's being harvested by every app on our phones. The average person checks their phone over 100 times a day. Each interruption fragments focus, and over time, we lose the ability to engage deeply with anything.

Almost everything will work if you unplug it for a few minutes — including you. — Anne Lamott

Small Changes, Big Impact

Start with one phone-free hour each morning. Eat a meal without a screen in front of you. Walk without headphones. Read a physical book. These aren't revolutionary acts, but they're revolutionary in a world that's designed to distract you.

The Productivity Paradox

We chase productivity as if it were a moral virtue. But rest, boredom, and unstructured time are not the enemy — they're the soil in which creativity grows. Some of the best ideas emerge when you're doing nothing at all.

Slowing down isn't a luxury. It's a necessary reset. In a world that never stops, choosing to pause is the most radical thing you can do.
